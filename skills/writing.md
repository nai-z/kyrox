# Writing

## Principles

- **Write for the reader, not the writer.** Every sentence earns its place or gets cut.
- **Active voice as default.** "The team shipped the feature" beats "The feature was shipped by the team."
- **Specific beats clever.** "Saved 3 hours per week" beats "dramatically improved efficiency."
- **One idea per paragraph.** If you can't summarize it in one sentence, split it.

## By format

**Email**
- Subject line: specific and scannable ("Follow-up on Thursday's call" not "Touching base")
- First sentence = reason for writing — no warm-up
- One ask per email — if you need three things, number them
- End with a clear next step or question

**Social post / caption**
- Hook in the first line — it has to stop the scroll
- Don't explain the joke
- CTA only if there's a real reason to include one

**Article / blog**
- Headline = the promise. The body delivers it.
- Lead with the most interesting thing, not the background
- Use subheadings so it can be scanned before it's read
- Conclusion = "so what" — what should the reader do or think differently now?

**Copy / marketing**
- Sell the outcome, not the feature: "Sleep better" not "Advanced sleep tracking algorithm"
- Use the reader's language — the words they'd use to describe their problem
- Remove every word that doesn't earn its place

## Tone matching

Match the register of what's asked:
- Formal request → professional, structured, no contractions
- Casual message → conversational, short sentences, natural
- Brand copy → consistent with the brand voice described in the brief

## What to avoid

- Filler openers: "In today's fast-paced world…", "I hope this email finds you well…"
- Passive hedging: "It could be argued that…", "One might consider…"
- Buzzwords: leverage, synergy, ecosystem, holistic, scalable (unless literal)
- Exclamation points used more than once per piece
